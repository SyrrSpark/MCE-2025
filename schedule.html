Event Schedule

Complete 2-day lineup of all Massachusetts Cannabis Expo events

1:00 PM - 2:30 PM

Speakers/Vendor/Sponsor/DJ Arrival Time

All vendors and sponsors can set up their booth, product activations, and brand displays.

Main Floor
2:00 PM - 5:00 PM

Massachusetts Cannabis Expo Job Fair

The MCE Job Fair consists of employers, RVTP vendors, expungement services, and resume writing workshops. Build your future in the cannabis industry.

Free Entry Featured Event Learn More
4:00 PM

Speakers Arrive

Each speaker will have a 10-minute podcast interview.

5:00 PM

Meet the Plug

The only network event that allows the cannabis community a chance to meet the cannabis cultivators in western Massachusetts. Although there aren't any sales or consumption, the public can still build direct relationships with the people growing the cannabis they consume. DJ, food, and refreshments included.

Free Entry Featured Event Learn More
6:00 PM

"Show Me the Money" Panel

Each speaker in this segment will have ten minutes to speak. At the end, the attendees will be allowed to ask questions for twenty minutes.

Panelists:

  • John Gilmore - SE Trust Fund Program Administrator
  • Kristen Glennon - BayCoast Bank Rep
  • Jill Grant - Sponsorship Collective
Free Entry
7:30 PM

"Battle of the Budtender"

BOTB provides a platform for budtenders to showcase their knowledge and skills in a competitive setting. Players must answer 20 questions correctly. The first one to answer all correctly wins. The grand prize winner will receive a BUD MASTER Champion belt and $2,500 cash.

Free Entry Learn More
8:00 AM

Speakers/Vendor/Sponsor/DJ Return

The Meet the Plug event continues from Friday.

10:00 AM

Commissioners Arrive

Commissioners are escorted to the Commissioners Lounge and briefed on the day's timeline, separate events, and goals.

10:10 AM

Commissioner Podcast Interviews

Each Commissioner has a 10-minute podcast interview with predetermined and approved questions.

  • Commissioners Bruce Stebbins and Nurys Camargo will answer questions around social consumption together
  • Commissioner Travis Ahern will answer questions surrounding the direction he plans to steer the Mass Cannabis Industry
  • Commissioner Ava Callender Concepcion's questions will be about the Suitability Standards
  • Commissioner Kimberly Roy's questions will center around HCA and her program
11:00 AM

THE MCE VIP NETWORK LOUNGE

Selected attendees will have the rare opportunity to participate in a private, closed-door roundtable discussion with Massachusetts Cannabis Control Commissioners, state legislators, top industry executives, and key stakeholders. This exclusive event offers a high-level, unfiltered dialogue about the future of the Massachusetts cannabis market.

Photo opportunities with key leaders of the western Mass community will be available.

Ticket Required Learn More
12:00 PM

1st Speaker: MCR Labs

Yasha Khan coordinates MCR Labs' marketing, outreach, and public relations efforts while also spearheading the organization's data science. He explains the challenges faced, including result manipulation by labs and the impact on public health.

12:20 PM

Commissioners Tour

Commissioners tour the Job Fair floor and meet with employers and employees.

12:30 PM

MCE B2B Lunch

Catered by Mi-Cultra 2 cafe and Jazzy Wings. Business Spotlight: Attendees have the option of introducing themselves and their business for one minute.

$50 Ticket Required View Menu
1:30 PM

2nd Speaker: Senator Adam Gomez

Senator Gomez will speak on the Clean Slate Program, a legislative initiative that aims to automatically seal or expunge certain criminal records for individuals who have served their sentence and remained crime-free for a specified period.

1:50 PM

3rd Speaker: Selia Williams, SEP Director

The Massachusetts Cannabis Social Equity Program (SEP) is a statewide initiative designed to create sustainable pathways into the cannabis industry for individuals most impacted by the War on Drugs.

2:45 PM

Commissioners State of the Industry Address

Each Commissioner has 10 minutes to speak to the attendees about the current state of the Massachusetts cannabis industry and its future. They will address general industry concerns, speak on new and old programs, and their individual projects. There will be no questions for this segment.

Note: All money raised during this segment will be donated to the Social Equity Program.

$25 Ticket Required Featured Event Learn More
3:35 PM

Meet the Commissioner Panel

This is a question-answer period where attendees text their questions to the host, who in turn relays them to the panel.

Featured Event
5:00 PM

Music Festival Begins

The DJ will start the night with 90's and 2000's R&B Music. From there we will transition into smooth Caribbean island music to set up for the old school Reggae artists that will carry on until 10:00.

Ticket Required
10:00 PM

Event Concludes

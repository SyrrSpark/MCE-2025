Saturday Highlights

Saturday features our premier events including the VIP Network Lounge, B2B lunch, and the highly anticipated Commissioners State of the Industry Address and Panel.

This is your opportunity to hear directly from the Massachusetts Cannabis Control Commission about the current state and future of the cannabis industry in Massachusetts.

Selected attendees will have the rare opportunity to participate in a private, closed-door roundtable discussion with Massachusetts Cannabis Control Commissioners, state legislators, top industry executives, and key stakeholders. 1st Speaker: MCR Labs

Yasha Khan coordinates MCR Labs' marketing, outreach, and public relations efforts while also spearheading the organization's data science. He explains the challenges faced, including result manipulation by labs and the impact on public health.

Yasha's Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) project aimed to gather testing data from 37 states, revealed significant discrepancies in potency and mold results. He will speak on this and other related topics.

2nd Speaker: Senator Adam Gomez

Senator Gomez will speak on the Clean Slate Program, a legislative initiative that aims to automatically seal or expunge certain criminal records for individuals who have served their sentence and remained crime-free for a specified period.

This gives individuals a "clean slate" to re-enter society without the burden of past convictions hindering their employment or other opportunities. It usually focuses on non-violent offenses.

1:50 PM

3rd Speaker: Selia Williams, SEP Director

The Massachusetts Cannabis Social Equity Program (SEP) is a statewide initiative designed to create sustainable pathways into the cannabis industry for individuals most impacted by the War on Drugs.

The program offers free technical assistance and training to help participants succeed in various areas of the cannabis industry, including entrepreneurship, managerial-level workforce development, entry-level workforce development, and ancillary support for businesses that contract with the industry but do not hold a license.
